Common Questions

Is my child's behavior normal or do they need help?
If you're asking this question, you're already demonstrating care and awareness. Many behaviors are developmental phases, but when they're causing distress for you or your child, or interfering with daily life, it's time to seek guidance. The free consultation will help clarify this.
Do you offer training for nannies and caregivers too?
Yes! Consistent approaches across all caregivers are crucial for success. We offer specialized training sessions for nannies, grandparents, and other caregivers so everyone is using the same effective strategies.
Can you come to our home in Ghayathi?
Absolutely. In-home training is one of our key services. Learning strategies in your actual environment makes implementation much more effective. We also offer clinic-based sessions if you prefer.
How quickly will I see results?
Many parents notice positive changes within 1-2 weeks of consistently implementing new strategies. However, lasting change takes time and practice‚Äîtypically 4-8 weeks for significant improvement. Every child is different.
What makes this different from advice I find online?
Professional guidance is personalized to your specific child and situation. Generic online advice doesn't account for your child's unique temperament, your family dynamics, or the specific challenges you face. You'll receive systematic, evidence-based strategies with ongoing support.
Do I need to bring my child to the sessions?
Initial sessions focus on training you, the parent or caregiver. This is often more effective because you implement strategies immediately at home. If needed, child assessment sessions can be arranged separately.
How many sessions will I need?
This varies based on your specific needs and goals. Some families see great improvement in 3-4 sessions, while others benefit from 8-12 sessions for more complex situations. The free consultation and initial assessment will give you a realistic estimate.
Is the initial consultation really free?
Yes! The 20-minute telephone or online consultation is completely free with no obligation. It's an opportunity for you to discuss your concerns, learn about the approach, and decide if it's right for your family.
